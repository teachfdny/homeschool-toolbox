The Record Keeper

I just signed up and I'm already seeing "Your Wrapped is ready" — is that normal?

Yes! If your school year starts in August, July is technically your year-end month — so you're getting a sneak peek of one of The Record Keeper's most exciting features right from the start. Your Wrapped is a shareable year-end graphic that captures everything you accomplished.

You don't need to do anything with it now — just add your subjects and start logging. It'll be a lot more fun to generate once you have a full year of data behind it.

My curriculum doesn't have numbered lessons. How do I fill in "total lessons"?

You don't need an official lesson-numbering system to use this field. Use whatever stopping points make sense for your curriculum:

  • Count chapters in your table of contents and use that as your "lessons" total
  • Decide how much you plan to cover per session, estimate how many sessions it'll take to finish, and use that number
  • Treat each unit or section break as one "lesson"

The field is just a way to track progress toward a total you define — it doesn't need to match a textbook's official lesson count.

Can I log lessons daily as I complete them?

The Record Keeper is designed for end-of-week logging, not daily tracking — and that's intentional. Just like many homeschool families plan at the start of the week, the habit here is to log at the end of it. Once a week, you record your totals: how many lessons you completed, hours spent, and a few notes on what you covered. That's it.

This keeps logging from becoming another daily task on your list. Five minutes at the end of the week gives you an accurate picture of what actually happened — which is exactly what you need for meaningful records.

How much detail should I put in the notes field?

Keep it short — think keywords, not sentences. The notes field is a memory jogger, not a journal. A few words that capture what you covered is all you need.

✓ Dividing fractions, order of operations ✓ Acids vs. bases, animal classification ✓ Terracotta Army, westward expansion ✓ Homophones, compound sentences

If you can read it back in six months and remember what happened that week, it's done its job. The goal is a quick, consistent log — not a detailed record of every moment.

Cloud Transcript Generator

How do I transfer my data from the free Transcript Generator to the Cloud Transcript Generator?

Export your data as a JSON file from the free Transcript Generator, then import it into the Cloud Transcript Generator. Your courses, grades, and settings will carry over automatically.

How does the course description builder work?

Add your courses in the generator and they automatically get added to the builder.

When should I do my course descriptions?

Ideally after your high schooler has completed a course. Curriculum changes happen, so don't spend time writing descriptions for courses you haven't finished.
